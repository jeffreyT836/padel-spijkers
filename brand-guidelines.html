01 / Brand Identity

Who We Are

Bandeja Padel Spijkers is where serious fun meets professional coaching. Founded by KNLTB-certified instructor Damian Spijkers, the brand brings tournament culture, expert lessons, and a tight-knit padel community to Rotterdam and beyond.

Brand Name

Bandeja Padel Spijkers

Shortened in-use as "Padel Spijkers" or "BPS". "Bandeja" is a signature padel overhead shot — choosing it as the name signals insider knowledge and craft.

Tagline

Toernooien, lessen en clinics.

Functional and direct. States exactly what BPS offers. In English contexts: "Tournaments, lessons and clinics."

Positioning

Community-First. Coach-Led.

Not a facility — a movement organizer. BPS creates the events, trains the players, and builds the network. Accessible to all levels, driven by expertise.

"Een levendige gemeenschap van padelspelers creëren waar plezier, competitie en persoonlijke ontwikkeling hand in hand gaan."
— Brand Mission · Bandeja Padel Spijkers

Value 01

Plezier

Fun is non-negotiable. Every tournament, lesson, and clinic is designed to leave players energized and wanting more.

Value 02

Kennis & Ervaring

KNLTB-certified expertise. Real court time in Barcelona. Coaching that comes from lived experience, not theory alone.

Value 03

Prijs / Kwaliteit

Professional-grade events and coaching at accessible prices. BPS punches above its weight — every time.

Do
  • Use Energy Green for exactly one focal point per composition
  • Pair Court Black with Off-White for maximum legibility
  • Use Lime Active only for motion/hover — never static primary copy
  • Maintain a 4.5:1 contrast ratio minimum on all text
  • Let dark backgrounds dominate — accents earn their impact through restraint
Don't
  • Place green text on green backgrounds
  • Use white backgrounds with this palette — it breaks the brand feel
  • Use both green and lime as equally weighted competing accents
  • Add blue, purple, or red to the palette without explicit approval
  • Use Mid Gray for interactive elements — reserve it for support copy only

03 / Typography

Type System

Two fonts. One display, one text. Together they balance athletic intensity with everyday readability — exactly like the sport itself.

Display Font

Barlow Condensed

Used for all headlines, labels, CTAs, UI components, and wherever typographic impact is needed. Condensed letterforms pack energy into tight space — like a padel court.

Barlow

04 / Logo Usage

Logo

The Bandeja Padel Spijkers logo is the primary brand mark. It must always appear clearly, without modification, on approved background surfaces only.

BPS Logo
Primary — on Court Black ✓ Preferred
BPS Logo
Reversed — on Energy Green ✓ Approved
BPS Logo
Light surface — on Off-White ✓ Permitted

Clear Space

Always maintain clear space equal to the height of the letter "B" in the logo around all four sides. Never allow other graphic elements to intrude on this zone.

Minimum Size

Digital: 120px wide minimum. Print: 25mm wide minimum. Below these sizes, use the monogram mark "BPS" in Barlow Condensed 800 instead.

Do Not Alter

Never stretch, skew, recolor, add drop shadows, apply gradients to, or place on a busy photographic background without a dark overlay.

File Formats

Use SVG for all digital contexts. Use EPS/PDF for print. PNG (with transparency) is acceptable for social media use only. Never use the JPG version on colored backgrounds.

05 / Voice & Tone

Voice & Tone

BPS speaks like a passionate coach with a dry sense of humor — knowledgeable without being elitist, energetic without being loud. Dutch directness meets court-side warmth.

Energetic

Sport creates momentum. Writing should too. Sentences are punchy, verbs are active, and there's no room for filler. Get to the point — then make it land.

Grounded

No hype, no promises we can't keep. BPS has real credentials — KNLTB certified, trained in Barcelona — let the substance speak. Trust is earned through honesty.

Inclusive

Padel is for everyone. Copy never talks down to beginners or gatekeeps from advanced players. Meet people where they are, and invite them up a level.

Direct

Rotterdam doesn't do fluff. Neither do we. Tell people what's happening, when, where, and why they should care — in that order. Cut anything that doesn't earn its place.

Community-Minded

This brand exists because of its players. Celebrate their wins. Highlight the scene. Make individuals feel like they belong to something bigger than a single match.

Playful (Earned)

Humor is welcome when it's genuine — never forced. A well-timed pun about padel is on-brand. Corporate buzzwords dressed as fun are not. Know the difference.

Do — On Brand
"Klaar voor je volgende toernooi? Schrijf je in voor het mixed event van 12 april — alle niveaus welkom, competitief speelniveau vereist."
Don't — Off Brand
"Kom jij ook genieten van een geweldige en unieke sportbeleving bij ons fantastische evenement?! Schrijf je nu heel snel in!!! 🏓🔥💪"
Do — On Brand
"KNLTB-gecertificeerd. Getraind in Barcelona. Nu actief in Rotterdam. Damian Spijkers coacht vanuit ervaring — niet vanuit een handboek."
Don't — Off Brand
"Wij zijn een professioneel en gepassioneerd team dat streeft naar excellentie in het aanbieden van hoogwaardige padeloplossingen voor iedereen."

06 / Graphic Language

Visual Elements

Abstract geometric forms inspired by the padel court, the racket grid, and the trajectory of the ball. Never decorative for its own sake — every element earns its place by reinforcing the sport.

Court Lines

Rectangular frame motif derived from the padel court layout. Use for section framing, content cards, and hero backgrounds.

Speed Lines

Horizontal motion lines suggesting the ball in play. Use sparingly on hero sections and tournament announcement graphics.

Diamond Grid

Rotated square form referencing the racket string pattern. Use as a background texture element or as a containing shape for icons.

Photography Direction

Favor action shots with motion blur, dramatic overhead perspectives, and close-ups of grip and footwork. Avoid posed group photos that look like stock imagery. Dark, high-contrast edits preferred — apply a subtle desaturation with enhanced greens for court tones.

Texture Usage

A subtle grain texture (noise: 3–5%, monochrome) can be applied over flat color backgrounds to add depth and tactility. Never use gradients that look like generic SaaS products (blue-to-purple, pink-to-orange). Gradients, when used, are radial and reference court lighting.

Social Media Guidelines

Caption Style

Lead with the key fact (date, registration open, result). Use line breaks, not emojis, to create visual separation. End with a single clear CTA. Maximum 3 hashtags — #padelrotterdam, #padelspijkers, and one event-specific tag.

Grid Consistency

Alternate between action photography and graphic text posts. Keep the dark-first palette consistent even on stories. Never post a fully white-background image — apply a dark gradient overlay if needed.

Stories Format

Use the full-bleed dark background with large Barlow Condensed type. Sticker links should use the brand green. Results posts: large score / name treatment + Court Black background.
